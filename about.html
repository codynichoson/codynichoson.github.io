About

I am currently pursuing a Masters of Science in Robotics at Northwestern University. This project-based program focuses on not just learning about, but actually applying robotics concepts to real-world systems. In just the first few quarters, I have had the opportunity to work on mobile robots, manipulator arms, a quadrotor, and a robotic hand. Upon graduation, I will have practical experience in robotic manipulation, mechatronics, navigation, control systems, and so much more.

Previously, I graduated from the University of Central Florida with a Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering with a minor in Intelligent Robotic Systems. My senior design project involved modifying an electric ride-on car for use by children with significant mobility impairments. The project was completed in partnership with the UCF Physical Therapy Department and the non-profit Go Baby Go! program. In the end, our team delivered a modified vehicle with joystick steering, proximity sensors, and increased ergonomic support to a partner family in need.

In the future, I hope to join a company with a creative vision and who utilizes robotic technologies to realize this vision. My current passions lie in robotic manipulation, computer vision, and mechatronics, but I am more than happy to take on any challenge that promises an exciting result!
